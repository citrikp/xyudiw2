Mari Herzer is a music producer, film composer, and synthesist. One of the starting points of her sonic practice is the attentive listening to the landscapes we inhabit, conceived as autonomous systems of rhythmic and melodic organisation, governed by their own non-human logics.

Herzer works at the convergence of electroacoustic and electronic music, and her compositions evoke naturalistic soundscapes. Through speculative techniques and unconventional readings of anthropology and biological science data, she crafts sonic ecosystems with specialised sound design for cinema.

For eight years, Herzer performed and was artist-in-residence at Mamba Negra, an itinerant electronic music party in São Paulo. Her debut album, Cheia [2022], was released on the MAMBArec label, where she also worked as curator and mixer. She has also released tracks on labels such as Osàre! Editions (UK) and TRATRATRAX (Colombia).

She composed the scores for República dos Juízes / dir. Eugenio Puppo (2022) and Sofia Foi / dir. Pedro Geraldo (2023), which won Best First Feature at the FIDMarseille festival.

Between 2025 and 2026, Herzer scored a short and a feature film, both supported by the Lei Paulo Gustavo. More information coming soon.
