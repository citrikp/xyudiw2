Mari Herzer is a music producer, film score composer and synthesist with over a decade of experience in electronic music — spanning commercial styles such as house, techno, drum 'n' bass, and tech house through the experimental territories of electroacoustic music and sound design.

With a sharp ear for sonic landscapes and their internal logics, Herzer crafts film scores and sound designs that balance creative originality with narrative precision — for advertising, cinema, and branded content.

A singular reference in the Brazilian market, she incorporates Amerindian perspectivism and natural science data as compositional tools, translating real ecosystems into precise electronic sonorities, with a particular sensitivity for projects addressing environmental, sustainability, and cultural identity themes.

For eight years, Herzer performed and held a residency at Mamba Negra, São Paulo's itinerant electronic music party. Her debut album, Cheia [2022], was released on MAMBArec, where she also worked as curator and mixer. She has also released music on labels including Osàre! Editions (UK) and TRATRATRAX (Colombia).

She composed the original scores for República dos Juízes / dir. Eugenio Puppo (2022) and Sofia Foi / dir. Pedro Geraldo (2023), awarded Best First Feature Film at FIDMarseille (France, 2023).

Between 2025 and 2026, Herzer composed scores for a short and a feature film, both supported by Brazil's Lei Paulo Gustavo cultural fund. More information coming soon.
